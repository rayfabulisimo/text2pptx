# Pan-Cancer Single-Cell RNA-Seq: Recurring Programs of Cellular Heterogeneity
## Slide Text with Bullet Points (6-8 words each)

---

## Slide 1: Title Slide
**Pan-Cancer Single-Cell RNA-Seq**
Identifying Recurring Programs of Cellular Heterogeneity

---

## Slide 2: Background - Tumor Heterogeneity in Cancer
**Tumor Heterogeneity Drives Treatment Failure**

- Cellular plasticity shapes tumor progression patterns
- Rare subpopulations drive metastasis and resistance
- Single-cell RNA-seq reveals intratumor heterogeneity directly
- Microenvironment interactions influence cellular diversity

---

## Slide 3: The Problem - Understanding Mechanisms
**Mechanisms of Heterogeneity Remain Poorly Understood**

- Functional implications of heterogeneity patterns unclear
- Model systems needed for mechanistic studies
- Do cell lines recapitulate tumor heterogeneity?
- In vitro models lack native microenvironment

---

## Slide 4: Authors' Novel Approach - Multiplexed Profiling
**Multiplexed Single-Cell Profiling at Pan-Cancer Scale**

- Pooled 24-27 cell lines per batch
- Profiled 198 lines across 22 cancer types
- Dual assignment using SNPs and expression
- Achieved 98% concordance in cell assignments

---

## Slide 5: Methods - Dataset Overview
**53,513 Cells from Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia**

- Average 280 cells profiled per line
- 10x Genomics Chromium platform used
- Detected 19,264 UMIs per cell average
- High quality data: 3,802 genes per cell

---

## Slide 6: Methods - Computational Analysis
**Non-Negative Matrix Factorization Reveals Programs**

- Identified 1,445 total expression programs
- Individual lines show 4-9 programs each
- Discrete subpopulations found in 11% lines
- Continuous cellular states more common overall

---

## Slide 7: Results - Twelve Recurrent Programs
**12 Recurrent Heterogeneous Programs Identified**

- Cell cycle G1/S and G2/M phases
- Three distinct EMT program variants
- Stress response and interferon programs
- Senescence, protein metabolism, and hypoxia programs

---

## Slide 8: Results - Cell Cycle Programs
**Cell Cycle Programs Show Context Dependence**

- G2/M programs identical across all contexts
- G1/S programs differ between tumors and lines
- MCM genes upregulated in patient tumors
- Histone H1 genes upregulated in cell lines

---

## Slide 9: Results - Stress Response Programs
**Multiple Stress Response Pathways Detected**

- DNA damage response with DDIT3 and ATF3
- Interferon response with IFIT1 through IFIT3
- Protein folding and proteasomal degradation pathways
- Programs reduced in MRE11A mutant lines

---

## Slide 10: Results - EMT Programs
**Three Distinct EMT-Related Programs**

- EMT-I specific to melanoma cell lines
- EMT-II observed in head and neck cancers
- EMT-III involves laminin and junction proteins
- Negative correlation with pigmentation program

---

## Slide 11: Validation - Programs Mirror Tumors
**Cell Line Programs Recapitulate Tumor Heterogeneity**

- Seven of ten programs match tumor patterns
- Gene signature correlation exceeds 0.7 overall
- Validated in melanoma and HNSCC tumors
- Combined analysis reveals shared cellular states

---

## Slide 12: Functional Study - Senescence Dynamics
**Senescence Programs Show Dynamic Regulation**

- Senescent fraction increases with passage number
- TP53 mutations affect senescence program prevalence
- Nutlin-3a treatment induces senescence program
- EpiSen cells transition to other states

---

## Slide 13: Therapeutic Relevance - Drug Sensitivity
**Senescent Cells Display Unique Drug Sensitivity**

- BCL family inhibitors selectively target senescence
- Navitoclax eliminates senescent cell populations
- Drug response correlates with senescence scores
- Senescence markers predict clinical outcomes

---

## Slide 14: Resource - Prioritized Cell Line Models
**Cell Lines Prioritized as Heterogeneity Models**

- Quantified RHP prevalence across 198 lines
- Matched specific lines to individual programs
- A549 lung line displays continuous EMT spectrum
- Resource enables targeted model system selection

---

## Slide 15: Conclusions - Key Findings
**Cell Lines Validate as Plasticity Models**

- Intrinsic plasticity exists independent of microenvironment
- Twelve recurrent programs shared with tumors
- Cell lines preserve tumor-relevant heterogeneity patterns
- Enables mechanistic studies in model systems

---

## Slide 16: Impact - Research Applications
**Enables Mechanistic and Therapeutic Studies**

- Experimental manipulation feasible in vitro now
- Drug testing in heterogeneous cellular contexts
- Heterogeneity biomarkers predict clinical outcomes
- Cellular state transitions are therapeutically targetable

---

## Slide 17: Summary
**Pan-Cancer Landscape of Cellular Heterogeneity**

- 198 cell lines profiled at single-cell resolution
- 12 recurrent programs mirror tumor patterns
- Cell lines validated as plasticity models
- Therapeutic relevance demonstrated through drug studies
